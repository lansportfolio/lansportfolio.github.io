How Do I Implement Keyword Research for my eCommerce Website

Published: 2025-12-08

In this project summary, I will detail the strategic workflow of how I implement keyword research for my Chinese tattoo design online shop. From one single seed term "Chinese tattoo" to a complete keyword list with hundreds of frequently searched terms, I conducted exploratory research, developed seed topics, tested multiple keyword mining tools, and classified keyword types. Each step was designed to support a single objective: to better implement content strategy by effective keyword mapping that drives sustainable organic search traffic.

Step 1: Strategic Discovery

I started the process with exploratory research to establish a baseline for the "Chinese tattoo" niche. I began by testing an initial seed term "Chinese tattoo" in Ubersuggest. For this project, I primarily used Google Keyword Planner and Ubersuggest, which I will reference in more detail later.

Testing seed term in Ubersuggest

Initial Findings for "Chinese Tattoo":

  • Search Volume: 5.4k/month, indicating a healthy niche.
  • Search Intent: Primarily Informational. Users typically seek inspiration or translations online before visiting a local studio for a custom design. This confirmed that a content-led SEO strategy was the most effective approach.
  • SEO Difficulty (SD): 47 (Moderate). Ranking for the broad head term is competitive, suggesting a need to target long-tail clusters.
  • AI suggested action: "Develop a comprehensive guide on Chinese tattoo meanings, styles, and cultural significance including FAQs optimized for featured snippets with schema markup." I decided to follow AI's suggestions, but first let's finish our keyword research so we can optimize content around keywords clusters.
  • Keyword ideas: Ubersuggest also provided related terms such as "Chinese tattoo dragon", "Chinese tattoo characters", and "Chinese word for tattoo", along with metrics including volume, intent, CPC, and difficulty, which is quite useful for early clustering and ideation.

Step 2: Defining the seed topics

To build a scalable content architecture, I transitioned from broad search terms to specific seed topics by categorizing user pain points and search patterns into four distinct buckets:

General user intent:

  • "Chinese tattoo meaning"
  • "Chinese tattoo ideas"
  • "Chinese symbols for tattoos"
  • "Chinese tattoo letters"

Meaning-based searches:

  • "Chinese tattoo dragon"
  • "Chinese tattoo for love"
  • "Chinese family tattoo"

Placement-specific searches:

  • "Chinese tattoo neck"
  • "Chinese symbol wrist tattoo"

Style-based searches:

  • "Chinese calligraphy tattoo"

These seed keywords served as the foundation for deeper data expansion using keywords tools.

Me from the future: Later I found out I could integrate Google Search Console (GSC) data to identify "High Impression, Low Click" queries, and capture existing "low-hanging fruit" keywords. But at this point I just started from scratch for this project, so let's save it for future iterations.

Step 3: Data Expansion & Tool Integration

With a defined seed list, I used Google Keyword Planner and Ubersuggest to expand each topic into hundreds of potential keyword variations.

Step 4: Keyword Classification & Priority Ranking

After compiling the final keyword list, I implemented keyword type classification and a Priority Matrix to ensure the highest ROI on content production.

1. Keyword Type Classification

Keywords were first divided into:

  • Vanity keywords (high-volume, broad intent)
  • Long-tail keywords (lower volume, higher specificity)

Long-tail keywords were further grouped into clusters such as character, meaning, gender, placement, and style, which were later mapped to blog posts and product pages.

2. The Priority Matrix

I assigned priority based on a weighted calculation of search volume and keyword type:

  • Highest Priority: Volume > 1500 + Vanity.
  • High Priority: Volume > 1000 OR (Volume > 500 + Vanity).
  • Medium Priority: Volume 300–1000 + Long-tail.
  • Low Priority: Volume < 300.

Note: Since this is a niche market, Keyword Difficulty (KD) remained relatively stable across the set, so I did not use it as a primary ranking factor. However, when high-priority keywords showed KD < 10, they were flagged as strong content opportunities.

Classified keyword list

Step 5: Keyword Mapping & Implementation Strategy

The final step was translating keyword insights into a scalable content structure.

My mapping strategy ensures that every page on the site serves a specific purpose in the SEO funnel:

  • Pillar pages: Each pillar page targets one highest-priority primary keyword and 3–5 high-priority secondary keywords.
  • Blog posts & product pages: Each page targets one medium-priority primary keyword and 3–5 low-priority secondary keywords within the same cluster.
